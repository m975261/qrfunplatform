Why this fixes your three asks
	1.	Centered: The outer <section> uses grid place-items-center, and the board has mx-auto aspect-square. No ml-* left shift; the table is always centered in the viewport.
	2.	Responsive: Sizes are driven by CSS variables and clamp():

	‚Ä¢	Board width: w-[min(92vmin,900px)] scales smoothly from phones to desktops.
	‚Ä¢	Avatars: --avatar: clamp(56px, 9vmin, 84px)
	‚Ä¢	Center area: --center: clamp(112px, 20vmin, 180px)
	‚Ä¢	Spacing: --gap: clamp(8px, 2.2vmin, 20px)

	3.	Relative positioning preserved:

	‚Ä¢	Avatar ring uses a single radius --r (percentage of board size). The 12/3/6/9 positions use calc(50% ¬± var(--r)), so everything scales but stays aligned.
	‚Ä¢	Nicknames, host crown, online badge, edit/kick buttons remain absolute inside each avatar container, so they travel with their avatar.
	‚Ä¢	Direction indicator and draw pile are anchored to the board‚Äôs own corners using --gap, not the page.

‚∏ª

Tuning tips
	‚Ä¢	Want a looser/tighter circle? Adjust --r (e.g., 36% ‚Üí closer; 42% ‚Üí further).
	‚Ä¢	Want bigger avatars on desktop? Raise the top bound in --avatar.
	‚Ä¢	If you introduce spectators or extra HUD, keep them outside the board <div> so the centered square remains clean.
